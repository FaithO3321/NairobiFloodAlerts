About Us

Nairobi Flood Alerts is a website born out of a collective desire to protect our beloved
city and its residents from the devastating effects of floods
. Our team comprises dedicated individuals with expertise
in disaster management, environmental sciences, and community outreach.

Our mission is to provide accurate and timely information about flood risks in Nairobi,
empowering residents to take necessary precautions and stay safe
. We work closely with local authorities, meteorological departments
, and community organizations to gather and disseminate reliable data.

Data Sources

Data Sources and Methodologies for Monitoring and Reporting Flood Alerts in Kenya

  1. Kenya Meteorological Department (KMD)

    Data Source: The primary source for weather forecasting and rainfall data.

    Methodology: Utilizes a network of weather stations across the country, satellite imagery, and advanced meteorological models to provide weather forecasts, early warnings, and seasonal predictions.

  2. Water Resources Authority (WRA)

    Data Source: Monitors water levels in rivers, lakes, and dams.

    Methodology: Uses hydrological stations, gauging stations, and remote sensing technology to measure water levels, flow rates, and catchment conditions. This data is used to assess flood risks and manage water resources.

  3. National Disaster Operations Centre (NDOC)

    Data Source: Collates information from various agencies and on-ground reports.

    Methodology: Integrates data from KMD, WRA, local authorities, and community reports to coordinate disaster response and issue alerts.

  4. Satellite Imagery and Remote Sensing

    Data Source: Satellites such as those operated by NASA, ESA, and other space agencies.

    Methodology: Uses satellite imagery to monitor weather patterns, rainfall, river levels, and potential flood areas. Remote sensing technology helps in mapping flood extents and assessing the impact.

  5. Global Flood Awareness System (GloFAS)

    Data Source: Provides global hydrological forecasts and monitoring.

    Methodology: Uses numerical weather prediction data and hydrological models to forecast flood risks up to 30 days in advance. GloFAS integrates meteorological data with river discharge and soil moisture information.

  6. Community-Based Monitoring

    Data Source: Local communities and volunteer networks.

    Methodology: Community members provide real-time observations and reports on local weather conditions, river levels, and flooding incidents. This grassroots-level data is crucial for validating official reports and enhancing local responsiveness.

  7. Geographic Information Systems (GIS)

    Data Source: Combines various spatial data sources.

    Methodology: Uses GIS technology to analyze spatial data, model flood scenarios, and visualize flood risk areas. GIS tools help in mapping flood-prone regions, planning evacuation routes, and designing flood defenses.

  8. Internet of Things (IoT) Sensors

    Data Source: Network of IoT devices installed in strategic locations.

    Methodology: Deploys sensors to monitor real-time water levels, rainfall, and environmental conditions. These sensors provide continuous data feeds that are analyzed for early warning and rapid response.

Mobile Apps

  1. MyDewetra

    Platform: Available for Android and iOS.

    Features: Developed in partnership with the Kenyan government and international organizations, it offers real-time weather updates, flood alerts, and disaster preparedness information.

  2. Weather Alert Kenya

    Platform: Available for Android and iOS.

    Features: Provides localized weather alerts, including flood warnings, based on data from the Kenya Meteorological Department.

  3. Red Cross Kenya

    Platform: Available for Android and iOS.

    Features: Developed by the Kenya Red Cross Society, the app provides emergency alerts, including flood warnings, first aid tips, and emergency contact information.

  4. Waze

    Platform: Available for Android and iOS.

    Features: Although primarily a navigation app, Waze allows users to report road hazards including floods. The community-driven data can provide real-time alerts and route adjustments during flooding.

  5. Safaricom's Maisha Alerts

    Platform: Available for Android and iOS.

    Features: Developed by Safaricom, this app provides emergency alerts including flood warnings, and integrates with M-Pesa for emergency donations and support.
