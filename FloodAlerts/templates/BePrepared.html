Be Prepared!

Floods can occur even when least expected. However, it is important to be prepared for such events. The following information can help endangered individuals to stay safe.

Flood Preparedness

  • Know your flood risk: Understand if your area is prone to flooding by checking flood maps and historical data.
  • Develop an emergency plan: Have an evacuation plan and designated meeting places for your family.
  • Prepare an emergency kit: Include essential supplies like non-perishable food, water, first aid kit, flashlight, batteries, and important documents.
  • Protect your property: Install flood barriers, clear drainage channels, and move valuables to higher ground.
  • Stay informed: Monitor weather reports and flood warnings from reliable sources.

Safety Measures During a Flood

  • Evacuate immediately if advised or if you feel unsafe.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters, as it can be deeper and faster than it appears.
  • If trapped in a building, move to higher floors or the roof.
  • Avoid downed power lines and electrical hazards.
  • Follow instructions from local authorities and emergency responders.
  • Stay away from areas subject to flooding until authorities deem it safe.

Impacts of Climate Change on Flood Events in Nairobi

  • Increased intensity and frequency of rainfall: Climate change is leading to more extreme precipitation events, increasing the risk of flash floods and urban flooding in Nairobi.
  • Rising temperatures: Higher temperatures can contribute to more intense rainfall and faster snowmelt, exacerbating flood risks.
  • Sea level rise: Although Nairobi is inland, sea level rise can impact coastal areas and potentially affect weather patterns inland.
  • Urbanization: Rapid urbanization in Nairobi, coupled with inadequate drainage systems, can worsen the impacts of flooding.
  • Deforestation: Loss of natural vegetation and wetlands reduces the capacity of the environment to absorb excess water, increasing flood risks.
