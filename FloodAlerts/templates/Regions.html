Regions Prone to Flooding

Based on reports and historical data, some of the wards and estates in Nairobi that are prone to flooding and were greatly affected by the recent floods in the city include:

Embakasi South Constituency:

  • Mukuru Kwa Njenga Ward
  • Estates affected: Mukuru Kwa Njenga slums, Sinai, Viwandani

Kibra Constituency:

  • Wards affected: Laini Saba, Lindi, Makina
  • Estates affected: Kibera slums (especially Gatwekera, Kianda, and Laini Saba villages), Lindi, Mashimoni

Dagoretti South Constituency:

  • Wards affected: Mutu-ini, Riruta
  • Estates affected: Kawangware, Riruta Satelite

Embakasi North Constituency:

  • Wards affected: Kariobangi North, Kariobangi South
  • Estates affected: Kariobangi North, Kariobangi South, Korogocho slums

Mathare Constituency:

  • Wards affected: Mabatini, Hospital
  • Estates affected: Mathare slums (especially Mathare North and Mathare 4A)

Westlands Constituency:

  • Wards affected: Parklands/Highridge
  • Estates affected: Parts of Westlands, Parklands

Kamukunji Constituency:

  • Wards affected: Eastleigh North, Eastleigh South
  • Estates affected: Parts of Eastleigh

Langata Constituency:

  • Wards affected: Nairobi West
  • Estates affected: Parts of South C and Nyayo Highrise

These wards and estates, particularly the informal settlements and low-lying areas, were severely impacted by the recent heavy rains and flooding in Nairobi. The floods caused displacement of residents, damage to property, disruption of essential services, and increased health risks due to the potential spread of waterborne diseases.

It is important to note that the flooding situation can vary based on the intensity and duration of rainfall, as well as the overall drainage infrastructure and preparedness measures in place.

Other new regions will be updated from time to time based on the amount of rainfall to capture new areas that could be flooded.
