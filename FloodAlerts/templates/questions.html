Frequently Asked Questions

1. What are the different flood alert levels, and what do they mean?

  • Safe: No immediate flood risk. However, residents should remain vigilant and prepared.
  • Warning: Potential for flooding exists. Residents in low-lying areas and near water bodies should take necessary precautions and be prepared to evacuate if advised.
  • Danger: Imminent flood risk. Evacuations may be underway or recommended. Residents should follow instructions from authorities and seek higher ground if possible.

2. How can I prepare for a potential flood?

  • Create an emergency kit with essentials like non-perishable food, water, first-aid supplies, and important documents.
  • Develop an evacuation plan and identify safe routes and shelters.
  • Protect your property by installing flood barriers, clearing drainage channels, and moving valuables to higher ground.
  • Stay informed by monitoring weather reports and flood alerts from reliable sources.

3. What should I do if I'm caught in a flood?

  • Evacuate immediately if advised or if you feel unsafe.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters, as they can be deeper and faster than they appear.
  • If trapped in a building, move to higher floors or the roof.
  • Avoid downed power lines and electrical hazards.
  • Follow instructions from local authorities and emergency responders.

4. How can I report a flood incident?

You can report flood incidents or seek assistance by calling our dedicated hotline at [hotline number] or by filling out the online reporting form available on our website.

Providing timely information helps us better assess the situation and issue appropriate alerts.

5. How does Nairobi Flood Alerts monitor and report flood risks?

  • We gather data from various sources, including meteorological departments, local authorities, and community reports.
  • Our team analyzes rainfall patterns, river levels, and historical data to assess flood risks.
  • We closely monitor areas prone to flooding and issue alerts based on our assessment of the situation.
